Policy 01 • Pay Structure

Flexible Benefit Plan

01
Flexible Benefit Plan
Pay Structure
Structural Change
How it works today
  • Fully flexible pool with 8 components: HRA, LTA, Car Lease, Vehicle R&M, Telephone, Meal Coupons, NPS and PDA.
  • You allocate across all components annually through myPay.
  • No floor on how much of your CTC is treated as Basic Pay.
  • No guardrail preventing your excluded allowances from exceeding the legal threshold.
What’s changing
  • Moving to a hybrid model — HRA, Conveyance, LTA, Telephone and Meal Coupons become fixed mandatory components.
  • NPS and PDA remain optional, as before.
  • myPay will auto-flag any declaration where excluded allowances exceed 50% of your Total Remuneration.
  • Some components will no longer be optional to opt out of.
  • Code on Wages S.2(y) requires wages to be at least 50% of Total Remuneration. Excluded allowances collectively cannot breach 50% of CTC.
  • Each excluded component must independently qualify as a valid CoW S.2(y) exclusion — not just be labelled as one.
  • Non-compliance inflates the statutory wages base and directly increases PF, gratuity and bonus liability.
  • The myPay guardrail is a compliance control, not a system preference. Its absence is an audit exposure.
  • No direct personal tax change from this restructuring alone.
  • If excluded components get reclassified as wages, they enter the PF computation base — increasing your employee PF deduction (12% of the higher amount).
  • Employees currently over-allocating to excluded components may see a small reduction in take-home once the guardrail is enforced.
  • If you are on the new tax regime, higher PF deductions are particularly costly as you receive no offsetting deduction benefit.
Policy 02 • Allowances

House Rent Allowance

02
House Rent Allowance
Allowances
Now Fixed
How it works today
  • Auto-set at 40% of Basic. You can flex it anywhere between 0% and 60%.
  • No automatic adjustment when you also opt for a Car Lease.
  • No notification if you are on the new tax regime, where HRA exemption is not available.
  • Deloitte recommended fixing this at 60% back in 2021.
What’s changing
  • HRA becomes a mandatory fixed component — removed from the flexible pool.
  • L01 to L05: fixed at 60% of Basic. L06 and above: fixed at 70% of Basic.
  • myPay will automatically reduce your HRA to 50% if you opt for Car Lease, to stay within the exclusion ceiling.
  • If you are on the new tax regime, myPay will notify you at declaration that HRA exemption is not available.
Grade-wise details
Grade BandHRA RateNote
L01 to L0560% of BasicMandatory — fixed
L06 and above70% of BasicMandatory — fixed
Any grade + Car Lease50% of Basic (auto-reduced)myPay guardrail applied
Company housingNilExcluded from HRA entitlement
  • A flexible HRA with no defined quantum can be challenged as not meeting the CoW S.2(y) exclusion standard.
  • Fixing HRA as mandatory with a prescribed rate gives it cleaner exclusion status and makes it audit-ready.
  • The guardrail keeping total exclusions within 50% of Total Remuneration is a direct CoW compliance requirement.
  • Employees in company-provided accommodation must receive nil HRA — the allowance cannot offset a cost they do not bear.
  • Old regime: HRA exemption under S.10(13A) is the lesser of actual HRA received, rent paid minus 10% of Basic, or 50%/40% of Basic by city. Receiving a higher fixed HRA without paying rent gives you no additional exemption.
  • New regime: HRA exemption is not available at all. Your entire HRA is taxable at your slab rate.
  • If your HRA increases due to the fixed rate, your tax position changes — particularly under the new regime.
  • Factor this into your tax regime decision during FBP declaration.
Policy 03 • Allowances

Leave Travel Concession

03
Leave Travel Concession
Allowances
Renamed + Fixed
How it works today
  • Labelled Leave Travel Assistance (LTA). Flat ₹1,00,000 per year for all grades L01 and above.
  • Paid only when you submit a claim. Unclaimed amount paid as taxable income at year end.
  • Optional within your FBP pool. Block 10 (2022–25) is still showing in myPay.
  • No restriction on LTC allocation if you also hold a Car Lease.
What’s changing
  • Renamed to Leave Travel Concession (LTC) across all policy documents and myPay.
  • Converted from a flexible to a fixed component.
  • Quantum: the lower of 12.5% of Basic or your remaining flexi balance, up to ₹5,00,000 per year.
  • Annual disbursement on mandatory bill submission. Car Lease holders at L06 and above: nil LTC allocation.
  • Block cycle updated from Block 10 (2022–25) to Block 11 (2026–29) in myPay.
Grade-wise details
CategoryLTC AmountPayment
All grades L01+Min(12.5% of Basic, residual flexi) — max ₹5,00,000 paAnnual, on bill submission
CLC holders at L06+NilVehicle benefit covers travel
Block cycleBlock 11: 2026 to 2029Update in myPay — action required
  • The Code on Wages uses the term Leave Travel Concession, not Leave Travel Assistance. A terminology mismatch can be raised in an audit.
  • Making LTC a fixed component strengthens its exclusion status under CoW S.2(y).
  • Nil allocation for Car Lease holders prevents double-counting of travel-related exclusions.
  • Block 10 still showing in myPay is a live compliance risk — claims in the wrong block lose their exemption.
  • Old regime: LTC exemption under S.10(5) covers travel within India for you and your family, twice in a four-year block. Only actual transport fare is exempt. Amounts paid without travel or bills are fully taxable.
  • New regime: LTC exemption is not available. The full LTC amount is added to your taxable income.
  • The block update from Block 10 to Block 11 is critical. A claim in the wrong block is treated as salary income in that year.
  • Annual disbursement with bill submission is the correct structure. Monthly payouts without bills are taxable salary.
Policy 04 • Allowances

Car Lease & Vehicle Allowance

04
Car Lease & Vehicle Allowance
Allowances
New Entitlement + Revised Limits
How it works today
  • Car Lease available for L08 and above with grade-wise thresholds.
  • Vehicle R&M available from L06 onwards. Current limits not revised in line with market.
  • No Conveyance Allowance exists for L01 to L05 — their entire CTC is treated as wages.
  • Policy does not explicitly state R&M is for expenses incurred in the performance of duties.
What’s changing
  • Car Lease for L08 and above is unchanged.
  • A fixed Conveyance Allowance is being introduced for L01 to L05, framed explicitly as reimbursement of expenses incurred in the performance of duties.
  • Vehicle R&M limits are increasing across all levels.
  • Policy framing updated to state the purpose clearly — required for the Code on Wages exclusion to hold.

  • Conveyance qualifies as a CoW S.2(y) excluded allowance only when framed as reimbursement of expenses incurred in the performance of duties.
  • Without any conveyance component, the entire CTC of L01–L05 employees is treated as wages, unnecessarily inflating PF and gratuity liability.
  • Introducing Conveyance at L01–L05 is projected to reduce gratuity liability by around 40%.
  • Vehicle R&M must also be framed as performance-of-duties reimbursement, or it risks reclassification as wages.
  • Old regime: Conveyance exempt under S.10(14)(ii) up to ₹1,600 per month when incurred in the performance of duties. Amounts above this are taxable.
  • Car Lease perquisite is valued under Rule 3(2) of IT Rules based on engine capacity and whether a driver is provided. This perquisite is added to your taxable salary.
  • New regime: S.10(14) exemptions are not available. Your full conveyance amount is taxable.
  • Vehicle R&M is exempt under S.10(14)(i) only with bills for actual official expenditure. Excess is perquisite income.
Policy 05 • Benefits

Meal Coupons

05
Meal Coupons
Benefits
Limit Increased
How it works today
  • ₹1,100 or ₹2,200 per month via Sodexo or Pluxee. Annual cap: ₹26,400.
  • Internal policy document incorrectly describes the tax treatment as slab-dependent.
  • Correct treatment: flat ₹26,400 per year exempt under the old tax regime only.
What’s changing
  • Annual cap increases from ₹26,400 to ₹1,05,600 per year.
  • Calculation: ₹200 per meal, two meals per day, 264 working days — aligned with Rule 15 of IT Rules 2026.
  • Meal coupons remain in the flexible basket. Sodexo and Pluxee delivery is unchanged.
  • Policy document corrected to remove the slab-dependent tax language.
Grade-wise details
DetailTodayFrom 2026
Annual cap₹26,400₹1,05,600
Per meal limit₹50₹200 (Rule 15, IT Rules 2026)
Old regime exemption₹26,400 pa₹1,05,600 pa
New regime exemptionNot availableAvailable — rare advantage
  • Meal coupons qualify as an amenity excluded from wages under CoW S.2(y) as a non-cash benefit. This exclusion applies regardless of the amount.
  • The slab-dependent language in the current policy document is factually incorrect and creates a legal risk if referenced in a dispute.
  • Correcting the policy document is a standalone action and should happen immediately.
  • Old regime: Rule 15 of IT Rules 2026 increases the per-meal limit from ₹50 to ₹200. Your new annual exempt limit is ₹1,05,600.
  • New regime: Meal coupon exemption under Rule 15 is available under the new regime as well — one of very few benefits that survive it. Maximise your allocation.
  • Opting for the full new cap gives you up to ₹79,200 in additional tax-free income per year.
  • Amounts above ₹200 per meal per day are treated as perquisite income under both regimes.
Policy 06 • Allowances

Telephone & Communication

06
Telephone & Communication
Allowances
Scope Expanded
How it works today
  • ₹36,000 per year (₹3,000 per month). Opt-in within the FBP pool.
  • Policy currently restricts coverage to fixed landline only.
  • Deloitte flagged the need to broaden this scope in 2021.
  • No bill submission requirement currently in practice.
What’s changing
  • Coverage expands from landline-only to mobile and broadband.
  • Converted from opt-in flexible to a mandatory fixed component for all employees.
  • Recommended limit: ₹5,000 per month (₹60,000 per year), or tiered by grade.
  • Bill submission required for tax exemption.
  • Telephone and broadband reimbursement qualifies as defrayment of special expenses under CoW S.2(y) and is excluded from wages.
  • Restricting coverage to landline in 2026 creates an unnecessary audit risk — modern work plainly requires mobile and broadband.
  • Without bill submission, the payment cannot be defended as a reimbursement and risks being treated as wages.
  • Old regime: telephone and internet reimbursements are fully exempt under S.10(14)(i) when incurred for official purposes with supporting bills.
  • New regime: S.10(14) exemptions are not available. Your full telephone allowance is taxable income.
  • Bill submission is the critical step. Without it, the exemption is unavailable even under the old regime.
  • Employees on the new regime moving from opt-in to mandatory will see an increase in taxable income equal to the full allowance.
Policy 07 • Retirement

National Pension System

07
National Pension System
Retirement
Contribution Rate Updated
How it works today
  • Optional for all L01 and above.
  • Employer contribution capped at 10% of Basic for all employees.
  • Budget 2024 raised the permissible employer limit to 14% for new-regime employees.
  • PepsiCo has not yet updated its policy to reflect the Budget 2024 change.
What’s changing
  • Employer NPS contribution increases to 14% of Basic for employees on the new tax regime.
  • Old-regime employees: employer contribution stays at 10% of Basic — unchanged.
  • NPS remains optional. It is not being made mandatory.
  • A communication campaign will follow to explain the tax benefit and encourage participation.
Grade-wise details

New tax regime14% of Basic (revised)S.80CCD(2)Up to 4% of Basic in additional tax-free income
Old tax regime10% of Basic (unchanged)S.80CCD(2)Deductible within the ₹1.5L combined S.80C limit

  • No direct Labour Code compliance requirement drives this change. The Code on Social Security 2020 preserves the NPS framework.
  • This is a tax optimisation step enabled by Budget 2024. PepsiCo is currently not passing on a benefit available since April 2024.
  • NPS must stay optional. A mandatory structure would reduce take-home pay and undermine adoption.
  • New regime: Employer NPS at 14% of Basic is deductible under S.80CCD(2). This is one of the very few deductions that survive under the new regime — and currently the most valuable tax lever available to you.
  • For an employee with Basic of ₹1,00,000 per month, the change from 10% to 14% means ₹4,000 per month (₹48,000 per year) in additional tax-free employer contribution.
  • Old regime: Employer NPS at 10% is deductible under S.80CCD(2). You can additionally claim ₹50,000 per year on your own voluntary contributions under S.80CCD(1B).
  • If you are deciding between tax regimes, the higher NPS rate under the new regime is a significant factor.
Policy 08 • Development

Professional Development

08
Professional Development
Development
Eligibility Updated
How it works today
  • ₹60,000 to ₹2,00,000 per year by grade. Bill submission required.
  • Eligibility clause reads ‘current role only’.
  • This is inconsistent with IT Act S.10(14)(i) and PepsiCo's own tax clause, both of which cover current or future roles.
What’s changing
  • Eligibility clause updated to read ‘current or future role’.
  • Aligned with S.11 of IT Act 2025 and Rule 15 of IT Rules 2026.
  • Quantum unchanged: ₹60,000 to ₹2,00,000 per year by grade.
  • Bill submission requirement stays. No change to the myPay structure.
  • PDA qualifies as defrayment of special expenses under CoW S.2(y), excluded from wages — provided it is incurred for work purposes.
  • The current role restriction is narrower than the law allows and creates a mismatch with IT Act S.10(14)(i) and PepsiCo's own tax clause.
  • Updating the eligibility clause removes an unnecessary restriction and makes the exclusion more defensible under audit.
  • Old regime: PDA reimbursements are exempt under S.10(14)(i) as expenses wholly incurred for the performance of duties, subject to bill submission.
  • New regime: S.10(14) exemptions are not available. Your full PDA amount is taxable if you are on the new regime.
  • If you are on the new regime, you receive no tax benefit from PDA — factor this into your FBP allocation.
  • The eligibility update to current or future role has no adverse tax consequence.
Policy 09 • Retirement

Provident Fund

09
Provident Fund
Retirement
Monitor Closely
How it works today
  • 12% employee and 12% employer contribution (8.33% to Gratuity Fund, 3.67% to EPF).
  • Compliant with the current EPF Act.
  • From November 2026, PF will be computed on the Code on Wages definition of wages, not the EPF Act definition.
What’s changing
  • PF rates are unchanged at 12% + 12%. No structural change.
  • From November 2026, PF is computed on CoW wages under the Code on Social Security.
  • If excluded allowances are not properly structured, more of your CTC falls within wages and your PF deduction increases.
  • This is why the FBP restructuring matters directly to your take-home pay.
  • Code on Wages S.2(y) defines wages broadly. From November 2026, PF contributions are based on this definition under the Code on Social Security.
  • Every component that is not a valid CoW S.2(y) exclusion will be added to the wages base and increase PF liability for both you and PepsiCo.
  • All salary restructuring must be completed and reflected in myPay before the November 2026 deadline.
  • Old regime: Your 12% employee PF contribution is deductible under S.80C within the combined ₹1,50,000 limit.
  • New regime: S.80C is not available. Your PF deduction continues at 12% but gives you no tax benefit.
  • If the wages base increases due to non-compliance, your PF deduction increases in absolute rupees with no offsetting tax benefit under either regime.
Policy 10 • Retirement

Gratuity

10
Gratuity
Retirement
Calculation Base Updating
How it works today
  • Payable at 15/26 × wages × years of service for employees with 5 or more years.
  • Fixed-Term Contract employees must complete 5 years before receiving gratuity.
What’s changing
  • The wages base for gratuity calculation updates to the Code on Wages definition from November 2026.
  • Salary restructuring across all levels will manage how the new definition applies to your payout.
  • For Fixed-Term Contract employees: gratuity is now payable after just 1 year of continuous service on a pro-rata basis.
  • Full and final settlement must be completed within 48 hours of your last working day.
Grade-wise details
DetailTodayFrom November 2026
Gratuity formula15/26 × wages × yearsSame formula, wages base updates
FTC eligibility threshold5 years1 year continuous service (pro-rata)
FnF settlement windowVariableMust complete within 48 hours
  • Under Code on Social Security S.2(88), wages for gratuity follow the CoW definition. An inflated wages base multiplies payout across the eligible workforce.
  • For FTC employees, CoW removes the 5-year threshold — gratuity is payable pro-rata after 1 year. This applies immediately.
  • CoW S.17(2) requires FnF settlement within 2 working days of separation. Late settlement attracts penalties.
  • Gratuity is exempt under S.10(10) up to ₹20,00,000 for non-government employees. Amounts above this are taxable as salary in the year of receipt.
  • For most employees the ₹20,00,000 ceiling is not reached. Senior employees with long tenure should review their projected gratuity.
  • Gratuity tax exemption applies under both old and new tax regimes — one of the few benefits that does.
Policy 11 • Separation

Severance & Final Settlement

11
Severance & Final Settlement
Separation
Process Change
How it works today
  • PepsiCo pays 3 weeks of guaranteed cash per completed year, minimum 8 weeks and maximum 40 weeks.
  • This is above the statutory floor of 15 days wages per completed year under the IDA.
  • Full and final settlement currently takes longer than 48 hours in practice.
What’s changing
  • Severance pay quantum is unchanged.
  • Full and final settlement must now complete within 48 hours of your last working day.
  • For FTC employees, gratuity is incorporated into every FnF after 1 year of continuous service.
  • HR Operations is redesigning the settlement process to meet the new legal requirement.
  • Code on Wages S.17(2) requires FnF settlement within 2 working days of separation. Late settlement attracts statutory penalties.
  • Code on Social Security removes the 5-year gratuity threshold for FTC employees — payable pro-rata after 1 year, must be included in every FTC FnF.
  • Statutory severance (15 days wages per year under IDA) is generally exempt from tax.
  • PepsiCo's enhanced severance beyond the statutory floor is treated as ex-gratia and is fully taxable as salary in the year you receive it.
  • Tax relief under S.89 of the IT Act is available when a lump sum covers multiple years. File Form 10E before your tax return to claim this.
  • Gratuity retains its separate exempt treatment under S.10(10) up to ₹20,00,000 — not affected by the ex-gratia treatment of enhanced severance.
Policy 12 • Mobility

Relocation Allowance

12
Relocation Allowance
Mobility
No Change Required
How it works today
  • 15 days of guaranteed cash (Basic plus Flexible Pay) as a one-time lump sum when you transfer.
  • Covers school admissions, resettling costs and installation of equipment and fixtures.
  • No bill submission requirement — the full amount is paid as guaranteed cash.
What’s changing
  • No change required under the New Labour Code.
  • Breaking the lump sum into specific reimbursable sub-components would improve your tax position.
  • Any revision to quantum or structure is a policy decision, not a compliance requirement.
  • A lump-sum relocation payment is generally excluded from wages under CoW S.2(y) as it is transfer-specific and not uniform.
  • If structured as guaranteed cash forming part of regular pay, its exclusion weakens. Sub-components with documented purposes are more defensible.
  • Under S.10(14)(i), relocation allowance is exempt when it meets expenditure wholly and necessarily incurred in the performance of duties — with actual expense and bill submission.
  • The current guaranteed cash structure without bill submission means the full amount is taxable as salary in the year of your transfer.
  • Restructuring into reimbursable sub-components with bills could reduce or eliminate this tax incidence for you.
