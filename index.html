Total Rewards Policy Updates

What’s Changing for You

India’s New Labour Code came into effect in November 2025. Below is a plain-language guide to every policy that affects your pay, benefits and allowances — what it means for you and what to expect.

Policy 01  •  Pay Philosophy

FBP Structure and Pool Size

01
FBP Structure and Pool Size
Pay Philosophy  •  Flexible Benefit Plan
Structural Change
How It Works Today
  • Fully flexible pool with 8 components: HRA, LTA, Car Lease, Vehicle R&M, Telephone, Meal Coupons, NPS, PDA
  • Employee allocates across components annually via myPay
  • No minimum floor on Basic Pay enforced
  • No system guardrail to prevent excluded allowances from exceeding 50% of CTC
What Changes
  • Move to hybrid model: HRA, Conveyance, LTA, Telephone and Meal Coupons become mandatory fixed line items
  • NPS and PDA remain optional flexible components
  • myPay guardrail auto-flags declarations where total excluded allowances exceed 50% of Total Remuneration
  • Certain components will no longer be opt-out
  • Code on Wages S.2(y): wages must be at least 50% of Total Remuneration. Excluded allowances collectively cannot exceed 50% of CTC.
  • Non-compliance inflates the statutory wages base and directly increases PF, gratuity and bonus liability.
  • A myPay guardrail is a compliance control, not just a system feature. Its absence is an audit exposure.
  • Each excluded component must be independently defensible as a CoW S.2(y) exclusion category.
  • No direct personal tax change from restructuring alone.
  • If excluded components are reclassified as wages, they enter the PF computation base, increasing employee PF deductions (12% of the higher amount).
  • Employees over-allocating to excluded components today may see a marginal reduction in take-home once the guardrail is enforced.
  • New-regime employees receive no tax benefit from most excluded allowances and are particularly exposed to higher PF deductions if the wages base rises.
Policy 02  •  Flexible Benefit Plan

House Rent Allowance (HRA)

02
House Rent Allowance (HRA)
Flexible Benefit Plan  •  CoW S.2(y) Excluded Allowance
Structural Change
How It Works Today
  • Auto-set at 40% of Basic. Employee can flex between 0% and 60%
  • No system guardrail when Car Lease is also selected
  • No regime-specific notification to employees
  • Deloitte 2021 had already recommended fixing at 60%
What Changes
  • HRA fixed as a mandatory component, removed from flexible allocation
  • L01–L05: fixed at 60% of Basic. L06 and above: fixed at 70% of Basic
  • myPay auto-reduces HRA to 50% when Car Lease is opted, to prevent 50% exclusion breach
  • New-regime employees notified at FBP declaration that HRA exemption is not available
Grade-wise HRA Structure (Post-Change)
Grade BandHRA Rate (New)Note
L01 to L0560% of Basic FixedMandatory — no employee choice
L06 and above70% of Basic FixedMandatory — no employee choice
Any grade + Car Lease50% of Basic Auto-reducedmyPay guardrail enforced
Company housingNilExcluded from HRA entitlement
  • HRA is an excluded allowance under CoW S.2(y). A flexible HRA with no defined quantum is contestable in an audit as it may not qualify as a uniform exclusion.
  • Fixing HRA as a mandatory component with a prescribed quantum gives it cleaner exclusion status and is more defensible under inspection.
  • The guardrail preventing total exclusions from exceeding 50% of Total Remuneration is a direct CoW compliance requirement, not a policy choice.
  • Employees in company-provided accommodation must be excluded from HRA entitlement as the allowance cannot serve a purpose it does not fulfil.
  • Old regime: HRA exemption under S.10(13A) is available on the least of (a) actual HRA received, (b) rent paid minus 10% of Basic, or (c) 50%/40% of Basic by city. Employees receiving higher fixed HRA but not paying rent will not get additional exemption.
  • New regime: HRA exemption is not available. Employees on the new regime will pay tax on the full HRA amount received at their applicable slab rate.
  • For employees moving from a low flexible HRA to the new fixed rate, the increase is automatic. Tax impact depends on regime and whether actual rent is paid.
  • New-regime employees should be specifically informed at FBP declaration to avoid post-payout surprise tax liability.
Policy 03  •  Flexible Benefit Plan

Leave Travel Concession (LTC)

03
Leave Travel Concession (LTC)
Flexible Benefit Plan  •  Formerly LTA
Rename + Structural Change
How It Works Today
  • Currently labelled Leave Travel Assistance (LTA). Flat ₹1,00,000 pa for all grades L01+
  • Paid on claim. Unclaimed amount paid as taxable allowance at year-end
  • Optional within FBP pool. Block 10 (2022–25) still showing in myPay
  • CLC holders have no restriction on LTA allocation currently
What Changes
  • Renamed to Leave Travel Concession (LTC) in all policy documents and myPay
  • Converted from flexible to fixed component for all employees
  • Quantum: min(12.5% of Basic, residual flexi balance), subject to ₹5,00,000 pa maximum
  • Annual disbursement on mandatory bill submission. CLC holders at L06+: NIL LTC allocation
  • Block cycle updated from Block 10 (2022–25) to Block 11 (2026–29)
LTC Structure (Post-Change)
Grade / CategoryLTC Quantum (New)Payment Mode
All grades L01+Min(12.5% of Basic, residual flexi), max ₹5,00,000 paAnnual on bill submission
CLC holders at L06+NilVehicle benefit covers travel costs
Block cycle (updated)Block 11: 2026 to 2029 Action RequiredUpdate required in myPay immediately
  • Code on Wages uses the term Leave Travel Concession, not Leave Travel Assistance. Using LTA in policy documents creates a terminology mismatch that can be raised in an audit or dispute.
  • Converting LTC to a fixed component strengthens its exclusion status under CoW S.2(y) as it becomes a defined, documentable allowance rather than an opt-in flexible item.
  • NIL allocation for CLC holders prevents double-counting of travel-related exclusions, which would weaken the CoW exclusion argument for both components.
  • Block 10 remains showing in myPay. This creates a practical compliance risk as employees may claim in the wrong block, leading to incorrect tax exemption timing.
  • Old regime: LTC exemption under S.10(5) is available for travel within India for self and family, twice in a four-year block. Only economy air fare or actual rail/road fare is exempt. Any amount paid without travel or bill submission is fully taxable.
  • New regime: LTC exemption under S.10(5) is not available. Employees on the new regime pay tax on the full LTC amount at their slab rate.
  • Block 10 to Block 11 update is critical. Claims made in the wrong block lose their exemption and are taxed as salary in the year of receipt.
  • Annual disbursement on bill submission is the correct structure. Monthly or lump-sum payments without bills are treated as taxable salary.
Policy 04  •  Flexible Benefit Plan

Car Lease and Vehicle Allowance

04
Car Lease and Vehicle Allowance
Flexible Benefit Plan  •  Conveyance + R&M
New Entitlement + Limit Increase
How It Works Today
  • CLC available for L08 and above with grade-wise lease thresholds
  • Vehicle R&M available from L06. Current limits not revised in line with market
  • Conveyance Allowance for L01–L05 is entirely absent
  • No explicit framing that R&M is for expenses incurred in performance of duties
What Changes
  • CLC retained for L08+ with no structural change
  • Fixed Conveyance Allowance introduced for L01–L05, explicitly framed as reimbursement of expenses incurred in performance of duties
  • Vehicle R&M limits increased across all eligible levels
  • Policy framing updated to explicitly state: "to meet expenditure incurred in performance of duties"

  • Conveyance Allowance qualifies as an excluded allowance under CoW S.2(y) only if framed as reimbursement of expenses incurred in performance of duties. Generic conveyance without this framing is treated as wages.
  • Absence of any conveyance component at L01–L05 means 100% of their CTC is treated as wages under CoW, unnecessarily inflating the PF and gratuity base.
  • Introducing Conveyance at L01–L05 is projected to reduce the Deloitte-projected ₹58 crore gratuity liability by approximately 40%.
  • Vehicle R&M reimbursements must also be framed as expenses incurred in performance of duties. Without this, they risk reclassification as wages under CoW.
  • Old regime: Conveyance Allowance exempt under S.10(14)(ii) up to ₹1,600 pm (₹19,200 pa) when incurred in performance of duties. Amounts above this are taxable.
  • Car lease perquisite valued under Rule 3(2) of IT Rules based on engine capacity and whether a driver is provided. This perquisite value is added to taxable salary.
  • New regime: S.10(14) exemptions are not available. The full conveyance amount is taxable for new-regime employees.
  • Vehicle R&M reimbursements exempt under S.10(14)(i) only when supported by bills for actual official expenditure. Excess amounts are perquisite income.

Meal Coupons

05
Meal Coupons
Flexible Benefit Plan  •  Sodexo / Pluxee
Cap Increase + Policy Correction
How It Works Today
  • ₹1,100 or ₹2,200 pm via Sodexo or Pluxee. Annual cap: ₹26,400 pa
  • Internal policy incorrectly states slab-dependent tax treatment
  • Correct treatment: flat ₹26,400 pa exemption under old regime only
What Changes
  • Annual cap increased from ₹26,400 to ₹1,05,600 pa (₹200 per meal, 2 meals, 264 working days)
  • Aligned with Rule 15 of Income-tax Rules 2026
  • Internal policy document corrected to remove slab-dependent tax language
  • Retained in flexible basket. No change to Sodexo/Pluxee delivery

  • Meal coupons qualify as an amenity excluded from wages under CoW S.2(y) as a non-cash benefit. This exclusion applies regardless of amount.
  • The policy document's incorrect slab-dependent tax language is a direct legal risk. Contradictory documentation can be used against PepsiCo in a tax or labour dispute.
  • Correcting the policy document is a standalone action that must happen immediately, regardless of whether the cap is increased.
  • Old regime: Rule 15 of IT Rules 2026 increases the per-meal limit from ₹50 to ₹200. The new annual exempt limit is ₹1,05,600.
  • New regime: Meal coupon exemption under Rule 15 is available under the new regime as well. This is one of very few benefits that survive the new regime and should be highlighted to all employees.
  • Employees opting for the higher cap receive up to ₹79,200 in additional tax-free income annually (difference between old and new cap).
Policy 06  •  Flexible Benefit Plan

Telephone and Communication

06
Telephone and Communication
Flexible Benefit Plan  •  Mobile + Broadband
Scope Broadening + Mandatory
How It Works Today
  • ₹36,000 pa (₹3,000 pm). Opt-in within FBP pool
  • Policy restricts scope to fixed landline only
  • Deloitte 2021 had already recommended broadening the scope
  • No bill submission requirement in practice
What Changes
  • Scope broadened from fixed landline to mobile and broadband
  • Converted from opt-in to mandatory fixed component for all employees
  • Recommended limit: ₹5,000 pm (₹60,000 pa) or tiered by level
  • Bill submission required for tax exemption
  • Telephone and broadband reimbursement qualifies as defrayment of special expenses under CoW S.2(y) and is excluded from wages.
  • Restricting scope to fixed landline when modern work clearly requires mobile and broadband is an unnecessary audit risk and easy to challenge.
  • Fixing it as a mandatory component with defined limits and bill submission makes it audit-ready and strengthens the CoW exclusion.
  • Old regime: telephone and internet reimbursements are fully exempt under S.10(14)(i) when incurred wholly for official purposes and supported by bills. No prescribed upper limit.
  • New regime: S.10(14) exemptions are not available. The full telephone allowance is taxable for new-regime employees at their slab rate.
  • Bill submission is the critical compliance step. Without it, the exemption is unavailable even under the old regime.
Policy 12  •  Mobility

Relocation Allowance

12
Relocation Allowance
Mobility  •  No Immediate Compliance Change
Advisory — No Mandate
How It Works Today
  • 15 days guaranteed cash (Basic + Flexible Pay) as a one-time lump sum for transferred employees
  • Stated coverage: school admissions, resettling costs, installation of equipment and fixtures
  • No bill submission requirement. Entire amount paid as guaranteed cash
  • Quantum appears below market compared to FMCG peers
What Changes
  • No Labour Code compliance change recommended by Deloitte March 2026
  • Restructuring into specific reimbursable sub-components (disturbance, school fees, luggage, accommodation) would improve CoW exclusion status
  • Bill submission against each sub-component would convert taxable lump sum into partially or fully exempt reimbursements
  • Relocation allowance as a lump sum is generally excluded from wages under CoW S.2(y) as it is not a uniform payment and is transfer-specific.
  • If structured as guaranteed cash forming part of regular pay, its exclusion status weakens and it risks being treated as wages.
  • Breaking it into sub-components with documented purposes strengthens the CoW exclusion argument for each component individually.
  • Under S.10(14)(i) of IT Act, relocation allowance is exempt when it meets expenditure incurred wholly and necessarily in performance of duties, subject to actual expense and bill submission.
  • PepsiCo's current 15 days guaranteed cash without bill submission means the full amount is taxable as salary in the year of transfer.
  • For a transferred employee at L05 with Basic of ₹60,000 pm, the lump sum of approximately ₹30,000 is fully taxable. Restructuring into reimbursable sub-components with bills could reduce or eliminate this tax incidence.
